A project where I improved the usability of the CBC-Radio Canada desktop and mobile Website.

Problem: The CBC-Canada Radio website, which is aimed at prospective employees, has some usability and responsive web design issues.

Solution: Redesign the Information architecture and page content to make the website more user-friendly.

My Role: I had one partner whom I collaborated with for the initial UI research and analysis. I completed the information architecture and redesign and testing individually.

UI Research and Analysis

Proto-Persona

To begin the project we created a proto-persona, Jacob, whom we imagined was the typical website user. We mapped out his behavioural demographics, goals, needs, pain points and some potential solutions to his frustrations so we could keep the end user in mind when researching and designing the website.

Jacob Eastmon, the proto-persona created as the typical website user.

Needs Statement

We then created a statement describing why Jacob visits the website from the proto-persona.

Jacob is looking for a new job as a news producer because he doesn't feel supported by his colleagues at his current local news outlet and hates that they focus on fake news and clickbait headlines. He is researching prospective employers and wants to find out about their values, the company’s history and what the current employees are like to see if CBC-Canada Radio is a place he would like to work.

User Path

Using the needs statement, we identified four goals Jacob wants to achieve when on the website and mapped out the path he would take to achieve these goals.

Goal 1

What current job opportunities are available?

Goal 2

Find out about the company’s history.

Goal 3

Find out about the company’s values.

Goal 4

Find out about the current employee experience at the company.

The user path for goal 3, find out about the company’s values.

Heuristic Analysis

A heuristic analysis was conducted to look at how easy the user interface was to use on five of the most important web pages.

The heuristic analysis on the ‘work with us’ section on the homepage.

Usability Testing

Five user tests were conducted (the number Jakob Nielson recommends to show 85% of usability problems) with the objective of discovering any usability problems when researching the CBC Website to see if you want to work at the company.

We set participants three tasks based on the heuristic analysis results to see how users interacted with the webpages we identified as having a lower standard of usability:

  1. Find out more about the history of CBC
  1. Discover what the company values are
  1. Find out what its like to work at the company

Usability Problems found from the testing include:

  • Unable to see a values page in the navigation bar
  • Confusion with non-clickable areas on the homepage
  • Disconcerted with the lack of information and lots of blank space on the About Us page, instead, the information is in a pdf that doesn’t have a contents page

User Testing Prioritisation Matrix

We gathered the testing results and put them on a prioritisation matrix for the user and company.

The highest priority issue for users and the business was to fix the navigation to the values page.

Information Architecture

Navigation Heuristic Analysis

To fix the most important usability issue found, the navigation to the values page, I firstly analysed the whole of the website navigation to analyse what the organisational principles were and discover any other usability issues.

Main Takeaways:

  • The organisational structure was hierarchal across the navigation
  • The logo on the primary navigation bar enables users to go to the homepage however they have to recognise the logo as the company name is only the footer.
  • The UI is clear with a hover function to let users know where they are navigating
  • The naming and structure within the secondary and tertiary navigation was unclear with some pages names duplicated but taking users to separate pages (Mandate, FAQ) and other pages missing and other pages in unexpected places, eg. Local News Directory page was in the Impact and Accountability Tab.

Navigation Usability Testing

Testing was conducted on the desktop and mobile websites to discover any issues when navigating the website and confirm that users struggled to find the values page – the highest priority issue found in initial user testing.

Tasks and Results

  1. Find the Our Values and Ethics Commissioner Page
    • All participants expected this page to be in the vision tab, not the impact and accountability tab.
  1. Go back to the Homepage
    • Participants used various ways to complete this task.
    • Either via the logo, the breadcrumb or by pressing return on the browser window.
  1. Find the Current Job Openings Page
    • All participants found this easily via the work with us tab.
  1. Find the main FAQ page
    • The Users in mobile view found the main FAQ page.
    • Desktop viewers expected the page to be in the secondary navigation not in the utility bar and either used search or went to the Ethics and Values Commissioner FAQ page.

Card Sorting

Open card sorting was done to redesign the navigation in three stages.

Stage 1:

  • Noted down all the pages in the navigation bar and some pages that should be in the navigation bar but currently not, eg. Our Values, on cards.
  • Renamed pages I thought were unclear, eg. Greening our Story was renamed to Environmental Strategy
  • Excluded some pages in the navigation bar that only took you to external websites, eg. #Not Okay. I would provide a link to this on a different page.
  • Combined pages that had limited content on them or only pdf content, eg. 2019-2024, 2020, 2015 strategy plans pages combined into one overall Strategy Plans page.

Stage 2:

  • Sorted cards I thought were similar into groups.

Stage 3:

  • Labelled Categories and sorted the groups further.

The three stages of card sorting, with a focus in on the new values group.

Redesign and Testing

Navigation Wireframes

As found in the heuristic analysis, the UI of the CBC-Radio website is consistent and has a clear design so I decided not to change the UI. Instead, I updated the mobile and website navigation bar with the new sitemap and also added the company name on the website version, an issue found in the initial usability testing.

The initial mobile and desktop navigation wireframes.

Homepage Wireframes

I began by sketching out the homepage design and then making low-fidelity wireframes, I kept it fairly similar to the original UI but did make some changes to make the experience clearer from testing results and heuristic analysis.

Changes made:

  • Iterated the desktop navigation bar to match the mobile UI
  • Added a heading in the news section
  • Changed the work with us template to make the individual pictures clickable and design more consistent with the rest of the elements
  • Added a carousel to the milestone section

The initial desktop and mobile homepage wireframes.

5 Second Desktop Usability Testing

I carried out five-second usability tests to gather the initial reaction to the UI from users.

Main Takeaways

  • The website site map made sense
  • Liked the navigation and button hover and clicking states and overall block style
  • Suggested making it clearer that the language is currently English and can move to French in the navigation bar
  • Suggested changing the copy in the milestones button to make clearer what users are going too

UI Style Guide

I created a style guide using atomic design principles to use on the website. I chose a sans-serif text for readability and kept the original black white and red colours to match the logo. I also made content cards and indicated whether the card was clickable with a red highlight on the text.

High Fidelity User Testing

After completing the high-fidelity wireframes I made them into clickable prototypes to do some final usability testing to identify any usability issues when navigating the CBC website and identify what users like and do not like about the design of the website.

Participants had four tasks:

  1. Please find the Trust, Democracy and Your Public Broadcaster Blog Page
  1. Please stay on this page and go back to the Homepage
  1. Find the page where you would discover what it is like to work for the company
  1. Please find and tell me the four company values

Iteration After Testing

I made the ‘CBC Canada-Radio’ text clickable on both mobile and desktop and ensured all the home buttons on the primary navigation directed to the homepage as a few users initially tried to click on the text to go to the homepage.

A screenshot of all the clickable links to the homepage on mobile and desktop.

I had feedback from testing that the content seemed a bit crowded. I believe this was a cause of the completion rates being lower on some of the tasks as users missed content when they scrolled past because there was so much of it. Therefore I iterated the UI with the aim to give better balance and make the content less overwhelming.

Changes made to give better balance and make the content less overwhelming:

  • Increased the spacing within cards
  • Decreased the text size in the CTA buttons
  • Increased the spacing within templates
  • Gave images in cards a fixed height
  • Blank space was introduced to keep card height the same in horizontal layouts

Screenshots of the milestones template on the desktop homepage before and after iteration. The spacing has increased throughout, the images are the same height and the CTA button text size has decreased to give better balance and make the content less overwhelming.

Next Steps
